## 2.1 Industry-Wide Weekly Patterns (Observed)

Based on direct observation of competitor LinkedIn and X profiles:

### Monday â€” "Start the Week Strong"

- **Examples:** "5 ways to optimize your scraping pipeline this week" / "Market trends to watch"
- **Observed at:** Bright Data, Oxylabs, Decodo, IPRoyal all post educational content on Mondays

### Tuesday â€” "Deep Dive Day"
- **Content type:** Product launches, feature spotlights, technical blog shares
- **Purpose:** Highest B2B engagement day (data-backed). Launch products here.
- **Examples:** "Introducing our new Dedicated ISP Proxies" / "Custom Parser deep dive"
- **Observed at:** Oxylabs and Bright Data both launch product content on Tuesdays

### Wednesday â€” "Authority Day"
- **Content type:** Industry news, case studies, PR/news mentions, comparison content
- **Purpose:** Mid-week peak engagement. Establish thought leadership.
- **Examples:** "Gartner recognized Bright Data" / "How eCommerce teams use pricing intelligence"
- **Observed at:** All major competitors post authority content mid-week

### Thursday â€” "Engagement Peak"
- **Content type:** Tips/advice, partnership announcements, webinar promos
- **Purpose:** Last high-engagement day before weekend dropoff. Best for partnerships.
- **Examples:** "Excited to announce our partnership with..." / "Register for our webinar"
- **Observed at:** Bright Data drops partnership announcements; Oxylabs promotes webinars

### Friday â€” "Human Day"
- **Content type:** Team/culture posts, community engagement, lighter content

# PART 6: MAKING IT LOOK ORGANIC

> **BLAZE:** This is critical. Nothing screams "new company trying too hard" like perfectly scheduled, perfectly polished posts at exact intervals. Here are the rules for looking real.

## 6.1 The Organic Content Rules

### Rule 1: Time Variation â€” Never Post at the Same Time
- **Do:** Vary posting time by 30-90 minutes each day
- **Monday:** 9:30am â†’ **Tuesday:** 10:15am â†’ **Wednesday:** 9:45am â†’ **Thursday:** 10:30am â†’ **Friday:** 11:00am
- **Never:** Post at exactly 10:00am every single day
- **Why:** Algorithms detect schedule patterns. Human social managers don't post at exact times.

### Rule 2: Content Mix â€” The 3-Post Rule
- **Never** post 3 promotional/product posts in a row
- **Pattern:** Product â†’ Educational â†’ Industry â†’ Product â†’ Community â†’ Tips
- **Maximum:** 2 product-related posts before inserting a value/educational post
- **Why:** Pure self-promotion kills engagement. Competitors like Bright Data mix heavily.

### Rule 3: Engagement First, Posting Second
- **Before posting:** Spend 10-15 minutes engaging with others' content
- **Actions:** Like 5-8 posts, comment on 2-3 posts, share 1 industry post
- **Why:** Platforms reward accounts that engage, not just broadcast. This also warms up the algorithm for your own post.

### Rule 4: Share Industry Content (Not Just Self-Promotion)
- **Ratio:** 60% original content, 25% curated/shared industry content, 15% engagement/community
- **Sources to share from:** TechCrunch, The Verge, Google Cloud Blog, AI research papers, developer blogs
- **Always add your take:** Never just reshare. Add 1-2 sentences of commentary.

### Rule 5: Imperfection is Organic
- **Occasional typos:** Don't fix every typo instantly (fix major ones, leave minor ones for 30 min before editing)
- **Casual tone:** Not every post needs to be a polished marketing piece
- **Behind-the-scenes:** Unpolished work-in-progress screenshots, casual team photos
- **Honest takes:** "We tried X and it didn't work. Here's what we learned." > "Everything we do is perfect!"

### Rule 6: Natural Language â€” Kill the Corporate Speak
- âŒ "We are pleased to announce our innovative new solution for enterprise data infrastructure optimization"
- âœ… "We just shipped something cool. Here's what it does."
- âŒ "Leveraging cutting-edge AI-powered technology to synergize data workflows"
- âœ… "We built a feature that makes scraping 3x faster. Try it."
- **Talk like a real person.** The proxy industry is full of corporate jargon. We stand out by being direct.

### Rule 7: Respond to Comments Fast
- **Target:** Reply within 2 hours during business hours
- **Weekend:** Reply Monday morning
- **Tone:** Helpful, genuine, not canned. Use the commenter's name.
- **Questions:** Answer directly. Don't redirect to "contact sales."
- **Criticism:** Acknowledge, don't defend. "Good point â€” we're working on that."

### Rule 8: Behind-the-Scenes Content
- Show the dashboard in development
- Share commit messages or release notes
- Post about problems you solved (not just the solution)
- Show real usage metrics (even small ones â€” authenticity > vanity metrics)

### Rule 9: Celebrate Others
- Congratulate competitors on wins (awards, funding rounds, milestones)

- **Why:** Generosity builds goodwill and looks confident, not threatened

### Rule 10: Platform Culture Matching
- **LinkedIn:** Professional but warm. 2-4 sentences before the list. End with a question. Use emojis sparingly (2-3 per post max).
- **Twitter/X:** Casual, punchy, opinionated. One-liners work. Memes are OK occasionally. Threads for depth.
